Hiking Essentials

Backpack

One of the most important things to have for a hike is a backpack. It is the simplest way to carry whatever gear you need. You can toss in snacks, your water bottle, a first aid kit for emergencies, and other things you believe to be essential to your enjoyment of the experience. Just don't carry too much or the weight will make your trip a lot more difficult.

Boots

A pair of good hiking boots will supply better support and protection for your feet as you're trekking along the trail. Also make sure to wear socks that will provide good cushioning, but are thin enough that you won't be sweating, which can increase the risk of blisters.

Other Gear

There are a lot of other things you can and should bring along on a hike to make it easier and more enjoyable. Hiking sticks can help you maintain stability and balance on more uneven trails, and they also make it easier on your body, reducing the stress on your legs by distributing the work across your whole body, since your arms are helping pull you onward with the help of the sticks. Many trails are well marked, but it is always good to have a map of the area and a compass so you don't get lost. Flashlights or headlamps for night hikes, or hats and sunglasses for sun protection during the day; there is so much you can bring, but it's really down to you to decide what you need to make your experience the best it can be!
